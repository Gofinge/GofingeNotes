2. Generation Process (Reverse Process)

A single step of reverse process

It is noteworthy that the reverse conditional probability is tractable when conditional on x0x_0x0​:
q(xt−1∣xt,x0)∼N(μ~(xt,x0),β~t2I)\begin{aligned} q(x_{t-1}| x_t, x_0) \sim \mathcal{N}(\tilde{\mu}(x_{t}, x_0), \tilde{\beta}_t^2I) \end{aligned}q(xt−1​∣xt​,x0​)∼N(μ~​(xt​,x0​),β~​t2​I)​
Using Bayes theorem, we have:
q(xt−1∣xt,x0)=q(xt∣xt−1,x0)q(xt−1∣x0)q(xt∣x0)∝exp(−12((xt−αtxt−1)2βt2+(xt−1−αˉt−1x0)2βˉt−12+(xt−αˉtx0)2βˉt2))=exp(−12((αt2βt2+1βˉt−12)xt−12−2(αtβt2xt+αˉtβˉt−12)xt−1+C(xt,x0)))\begin{aligned} q(x_{t-1}| x_t, x_0) &= q(x_t|x_{t-1}, x_0)\frac{q(x_{t-1}| x_0)}{q(x_t|x_0)} \\ &\propto \text{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(\frac{(x_t - \alpha_t x_{t-1})^2}{\beta_t^2} + \frac{(x_{t-1} - \bar{\alpha}_{t-1}x_0)^2}{\bar{\beta}_{t-1}^2} + \frac{(x_{t} - \bar{\alpha}_{t}x_0)^2}{\bar{\beta}_{t}^2})) \\ &= \text{exp}(-\frac{1}{2}(({\color{red}{\frac{\alpha_t^2}{\beta_t^2} + \frac{1}{\bar{\beta}_{t-1}^2}}})x_{t-1}^2 - 2({\color{blue}{\frac{\alpha_t}{\beta_t^2}x_t + \frac{\bar{\alpha}_t}{\bar{\beta}_{t-1}^2}}})x_{t-1} + C(x_t, x_0))) \end{aligned}q(xt−1​∣xt​,x0​)​=q(xt​∣xt−1​,x0​)q(xt​∣x0​)q(xt−1​∣x0​)​∝exp(−21​(βt2​(xt​−αt​xt−1​)2​+βˉ​t−12​(xt−1​−αˉt−1​x0​)2​+βˉ​t2​(xt​−αˉt​x0​)2​))=exp(−21​((βt2​αt2​​+βˉ​t−12​1​)xt−12​−2(βt2​αt​​xt​+βˉ​t−12​αˉt​​)xt−1​+C(xt​,x0​)))​

Following the standard Gaussian density function, the mean and variance can be parameterized as follow (recall that αt2+βt2=1\alpha_t^2 + \beta_t^2 = 1αt2​+βt2​=1 and αˉt2+βˉt2=1\bar{\alpha}_t^2 + \bar{\beta}_t^2 = 1αˉt2​+βˉ​t2​=1):
